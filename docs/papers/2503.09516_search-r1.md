# Search-R1: Training LLMs to Reason and Leverage Search Engines with Reinforcement Learning

> **Authors**: Bowen Jin, Hansi Zeng, Zhenrui Yue, Jinsung Yoon, Sercan Arik, Dong Wang, Hamed Zamani, Jiawei Han.

## TL;DR

- Trains an LLM to interleave reasoning with search-engine calls using outcome-only EM reward, no SFT, no process supervision.
- PPO is the default; GRPO is also tested. Loss-masks retrieved tokens (the headline ablation in §5.4 reports "~25% average relative gain" from masking).
- Trained on a unified NQ + HotpotQA mix at Qwen2.5-3B / 7B / 14B (Base + Instruct each).
- Evaluated on **7 benchmarks** (NQ, TriviaQA, PopQA, HotpotQA, 2Wiki, MuSiQue, Bamboogle).
- **This is our M1 reproduction target.** The published GRPO checkpoints (`PeterJinGo/SearchR1-nq_hotpotqa_train-qwen2.5-3b-(it-)em-grpo`) are what we reproduce on 7 benchmarks; see [`../report/RESULTS_m1.md`](../report/RESULTS_m1.md).

## Problem

RAG and prompt-engineered search agents either rely on hand-crafted prompts (brittle) or on supervised intermediate traces (expensive). The paper asks whether outcome-only RL can teach a model to call a search engine multiple times during a single answer.

## Method

- **Algorithm**: PPO (default) and GRPO. Section 5.1 ablates the two; both work, paper headlines GRPO results in Table 3.
- **Tag schema**: `<search>...</search>` for retrieval queries, `<information>...</information>` for retrieved chunks (inserted by the environment), `<answer>...</answer>` for final answer.
- **Reward**: rule-based, outcome-only. `r(x, y) = EM(a_pred, a_gold)`. No format reward, no process reward, no neural reward model. The paper explicitly avoids both.
- **Loss masking**: indicator `I(y_t) = 1` if `y_t` is LLM-generated, `0` if retrieved. Policy gradient is computed only over generated tokens. Section 5.4: ablation shows ~25% average relative gain from masking.
- **Retrieval setup**: 2018 Wikipedia dump, E5 retriever, **top-k = 3** by default (k=1, 5 ablated in Appendix G).

## Training setup

(From paper §4 and the [official repo](https://github.com/PeterGriffinJin/Search-R1).)

- **Base models tested**: Qwen2.5-3B (Base + Instruct), Qwen2.5-7B (Base + Instruct), Qwen2.5-14B (Base + Instruct, Appendix C).
- **GPUs**: "Training is performed on a single node with 8 H100 GPUs" (paper §4).
- **Distributed config**: FSDP with CPU offloading; gradient checkpointing; vLLM with tensor-parallel size 1, GPU memory utilization 0.6.
- **Batch sizes**: total batch 512, mini-batch 256, micro-batch 64.
- **Sequence**: max sequence length 4,096 tokens.
- **Schedule**: 500 steps total; checkpoints every 100 steps.
- **Wall-clock**: not stated.
- **GRPO group size (G)**: ablated 1, 3, 5 in Appendix H. Headline G not stated explicitly here; check appendix.

## Data

- **Train**: merged NQ + HotpotQA training sets ("unified dataset for Search-R1 and other fine-tuning based baselines"). Exact size not in our extract.
- **Eval (7 benchmarks)**:
  - In-domain: NQ, HotpotQA.
  - Out-of-domain: TriviaQA, PopQA, 2WikiMultiHopQA, MuSiQue, Bamboogle.
- **Metric**: EM (exact match) on the final answer.

## Takeaways for us

- **This paper is the M1 baseline** in our project. Plan B v1 reproduction landed within ±2.5 pp of the paper across the 7 benchmarks at 3B (base avg 0.292 vs 0.312; instruct avg 0.361 vs 0.336). See [`../report/RESULTS_m1.md`](../report/RESULTS_m1.md).
- **Outcome-only EM reward**: contrasts with ReSearch's F1 + 0.1 partial-credit and R1-Searcher's two-stage F1 + asymmetric format penalty. The paper avoids any format component and gets stable training. Useful counter-evidence to ReSearch's design.
- **Retrieved-token masking is the most ablated trick of the three papers** (§5.4 reports a quantified gain). All three papers mask retrieved tokens; only Search-R1 publishes the magnitude.
- **8 H100 single-node** is the most concrete compute claim of the three. Search-R1's shape (b=512, mb=256, mu=64, len=4096, 500 steps) is what our [Phase-2 NeMo-RL training](../training/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md) ports to NeMo-RL because verl does not support Qwen3.5.
- **Top-k = 3** is half of ReSearch's 5. Worth noting when comparing retrieval call counts.

## Limitations

- Authors admit (paper §6 / conclusion): single Wikipedia corpus; outcome-only reward may not generalise to long-form or subjective answers; no process supervision means harder to debug failures.
- We add: "single node 8 H100" is concrete on count and type but wall-clock and total training tokens are not stated. EM-only reward is sensitive to answer canonicalisation; see [`../eval/PAPER_VS_OURS_AUDIT.md`](../eval/PAPER_VS_OURS_AUDIT.md) for the 8 divergences (D1-D8) we found between the official repo behaviour and our ports.

## Open questions / followups

- Where is "GRPO group size G" set in the published training config? Appendix H ablates {1,3,5}; what is the headline?
- Compare GRPO vs PPO (paper §5.1) on our reproduction; we have only run GRPO checkpoints.
- Retrieved-token masking is reported to give "~25% average relative gain". Does that hold at our 0.6B / 2B scale? Cheap to ablate.
