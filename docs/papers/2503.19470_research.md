# ReSearch: Learning to Reason with Search for LLMs via Reinforcement Learning

> **Authors**: Mingyang Chen, Linzhuang Sun, Tianpeng Li, Haoze Sun, Yijie Zhou, Chenzheng Zhu, Haofen Wang, Jeff Z. Pan, Wen Zhang, Huajun Chen, Fan Yang, Zenan Zhou, Weipeng Chen.
> **Venue**: NeurIPS 2025 (accepted).
> **Version read**: arXiv 2503.19470v3 (2025-09-23); v3 is the camera-ready, supersedes v1 (2025-03-25) and v2 (2025-03-27).
> **Links**: [arxiv abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19470) - [arxiv html](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.19470v3) - [alphaxiv](https://www.alphaxiv.org/abs/2503.19470) - [wayback abs](https://web.archive.org/web/2026*/https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19470) - [code (re-search branch)](https://github.com/Agent-RL/ReCall/tree/re-search) - [code (current, ReCall)](https://github.com/Agent-RL/ReCall) - [wayback code](https://web.archive.org/web/2026*/https://github.com/Agent-RL/ReCall) - [local PDF](../raw/papers/2026-05-06_research-rl.pdf)

## TL;DR

- GRPO with a simple F1 + format-bonus reward is enough to teach Qwen2.5 (base or instruct) to interleave reasoning with retrieval calls. No SFT, no reasoning supervision.
- Trained on **MuSiQue alone** (~20k samples); evaluated on HotpotQA, 2Wiki, MuSiQue, Bamboogle. Strong OOD generalisation despite single-dataset training.
- Tag schema is `<think>...</think> <search>...</search> <result>...</result>`. Retrieved tokens are masked from the policy gradient (asserted, not ablated in the paper).
- **This is the recipe behind our Phase-1 [v0](../report/RESULTS_v0.md) and [v1](../report/RESULTS_v1.md) results** on Qwen3-0.6B (29 runs total). Paper used 7B/32B on 8×8 H800; we ran 0.6B on ALICE (per [`../report/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md`](../report/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md)). The recipe held qualitatively but the partial-credit reward created a 0.1 floor that masked tool-use signal.

## Problem

Multi-step RAG agents either rely on hand-written prompts (brittle, do not transfer) or on supervised intermediate reasoning traces (expensive, and hard to label correctly for tool-use). Can outcome-reward RL alone induce useful retrieval behaviour and a chain of reasoning?

## Method

- **Algorithm**: vanilla GRPO with reference-model KL.
- **Tag schema**: `<think>...</think>` for reasoning, `<search>query</search>` for retrieval calls, `<result>...</result>` for retrieved chunks (inserted by the environment), final answer in `\boxed{...}`.
- **Reward** (rule-based, two components):
  - **Answer reward**: F1 between the `\boxed{}` content and the gold answer when F1 > 0.
  - **Format reward**: 0.1 when F1 = 0 but the trajectory has the correct tag structure; 0 otherwise.
  - This is the "partial-credit" reward we found problematic; see "Takeaways for us".
- **Loss masking**: tokens inside `<result>...</result>` are masked from the policy gradient. Asserted in the paper; not ablated.
- **Retrieval setup**: E5-base-v2 dense retriever over Wikipedia (December 2018 snapshot) via the **FlashRAG** toolkit. Top-k = 5.

## Training setup

(Pulled from arXiv 2503.19470v3 §4.2 and `scripts/train/train_multi_node.sh` on the [`re-search` branch of `Agent-RL/ReCall`](https://github.com/Agent-RL/ReCall/tree/re-search). The original `Agent-RL/ReSearch` repo has been merged into `Agent-RL/ReCall`; the paper-faithful config lives on the `re-search` branch.)

-  **Base models**: `Qwen2.5-7B`, `Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct`, `Qwen2.5-32B`, `Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct`.
- **GPUs**: **64× H800** (8 nodes × 8 H800 per node). Stated in the paper. The published `train_multi_node.sh` defaults to `NNODES=1`, `N_GPUS_PER_NODE=8` (single-node 8-GPU); users override NNODES for the paper config.
- **Distributed config**: full-parameter optimisation, gradient checkpointing, vLLM tensor-parallel size 2 for rollouts.
- **Batch sizes**: `train_batch_size=256`, `ppo_mini_batch_size=64`, **`rollout_n=5`** (G=5; same as Search-R1, far below R1-Searcher's G=16).
- **Optimisation**: actor LR `1e-6` (lower than R1-Searcher's `2e-6`), 2 epochs.
- **Sequence**: `max_prompt_length=1536`, `max_response_length=6656` (conservative compared to R1-Searcher's 29k generate-max). `max_turns=5` retrieval calls per trajectory.
- **Schedule**: 2 epochs over the MuSiQue training set; `save_freq=10`, `test_freq=10` epochs.
- **Wall-clock**: not stated in paper or scripts.
- **Reward**: rule-based, in-process, no separate reward server.

## Data

- **Train**: MuSiQue training set only (19,938 samples). The paper emphasises this single-dataset choice "since it has various types of multi-hop questions". Compare R1-Searcher's HotpotQA + 2Wiki mix at ~8k samples.
- **Eval**: HotpotQA (7,405 dev), 2WikiMultiHopQA (12,576 dev), MuSiQue (2,417 dev), Bamboogle (125 test).
- **Metrics**: Exact Match (EM) and LLM-as-Judge (LJ) using GPT-4o-mini. Both reported.

## Results

(arXiv 2503.19470v3, Table 3; Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct row.)

| Benchmark | EM | LJ | vs strongest baseline |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| HotpotQA | 46.73 | 67.70 | +14-16 pp |
| 2WikiMultiHopQA | 44.90 | 50.30 | +14-16 pp |
| MuSiQue (in-distribution) | 26.40 | 38.56 | +14-16 pp |
| Bamboogle (OOD) | 56.80 | 67.20 | +14-16 pp |

Average improvements: 14.82-15.81 pp EM and 15.46-17.56 pp LJ across model sizes. Both response length and average search calls per trajectory grow during training.

## Takeaways for us

This is the **paper our [Phase-1 v0/v1 Qwen3-0.6B results](../report/RESULTS_v0.md) port to small scale**. The recipe is what we replicated on ALICE with 29 runs total. Findings that matter for our project:

- **Recipe transfers qualitatively to 0.6B** (instruct), but rewards cluster at 0.18-0.22. The paper's headline EM (e.g. 46.73 on HotpotQA, Table 3) is the Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct row, not 7B; per-size 7B numbers should be read from the paper directly when comparing.
- **Base model cannot bootstrap from cold-start at 0.6B** (5/5 our v1 base attempts stayed at 0 tool calls; longest 2300 steps). The paper does it on 7B/32B via GRPO alone; at 0.6B GRPO alone is not enough. R1-Searcher's [2-stage curriculum](2503.05592_r1-searcher.md#method) is a proposed fix.
- **The 0.1 partial-credit reward creates a noise floor** that masks tool-use signal. We measured a 3-6 pp gap between tool-using and no-tool runs at the same final reward (gotcha #4 in [`../../.claude/CLAUDE.md`](../../.claude/CLAUDE.md)). The most actionable lever surfaced from Phase-1.
- **Tag schema is interchangeable**: in v1 we swapped `<search>...</search>` for in-distribution `<tool_call>` tags at no cost in step count. Confirms the policy is learning *structure*, not the literal token strings.
- **64 H800 vs our 1 A100**: their stated compute is 64× ours by GPU count alone. Direct convergence wall-clock comparison would need a measurement; our [Phase-2 wall-clock](../training/SMOKE_RESULTS_2026-05-06.md) of 11-17 days for a Search-R1-shape Qwen3.5-2B run on 1× A100 is for that shape, not ReSearch's.
- **Single-dataset MuSiQue training** generalises well in their hands. We have not tried this exact split; our v0/v1 used a different mix. Worth a smoke run.

## Limitations

- Authors admit: assumes verifiable answers; limited to Wikipedia; no reward-design ablation; no retrieve-mask ablation.
- We add: the partial-credit reward is the load-bearing detail and is presented in the paper without comment; it is the most ablation-worthy line in the loss. The fact that the paper does not ablate it is the **biggest gap to exploit** for our thesis.
- Compute is partially stated (8×8 H800) but wall-clock is missing.
- LLM-as-Judge with GPT-4o-mini is an evaluation dependency we should not adopt for the thesis; stick to EM/F1 for reproducibility.

## Open questions / followups

- Replace the 0.1 floor with a binary 0/1 reward; does tool-use signal sharpen? This is in the active ablation list ([`../TODO_2026-05-04.md`](../TODO_2026-05-04.md)).
- Single-dataset MuSiQue training vs HotpotQA + 2Wiki mix at our scale; does the OOD generalisation hold?
- 7B vs 0.6B vs 2B: where does the cold-start failure mode disappear? Smoke-test on 2B with the paper recipe before adding tricks.
- Does retrieve-mask matter? Cheap to ablate (single boolean in the loss).
