# R1-Searcher: Incentivizing the Search Capability in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning

> **Authors**: Huatong Song, Jinhao Jiang, Yingqian Min, Jie Chen, Zhipeng Chen, Wayne Xin Zhao, Lei Fang, Ji-Rong Wen (Gaoling School of AI, Renmin University of China; DataCanvas Alaya NeW).
> **Version read**: arXiv 2503.05592v2 (2025-03-18); v2 is the most recent listed at ingest time.
> **Links**: [arxiv abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.05592) - [arxiv html](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.05592v2) - [alphaxiv](https://www.alphaxiv.org/abs/2503.05592) - [wayback abs](https://web.archive.org/web/2026*/https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.05592) - [code](https://github.com/RUCAIBox/R1-Searcher) - [wayback code](https://web.archive.org/web/2026*/https://github.com/RUCAIBox/R1-Searcher) - [local PDF](../raw/papers/2026-05-06_r1-searcher.pdf)

## TL;DR

- Two-stage outcome-RL recipe that teaches a base LLM to interleave reasoning with retrieval queries, no SFT or distillation required.
- Stage 1 rewards calling the retriever at all (and using the right tags); Stage 2 rewards F1 on the final answer with a stricter format penalty.
- Algorithm is Reinforce++ (not GRPO) with two custom hooks: a RAG-rollout that pauses generation at the search tag, and retrieve-mask loss that zeroes the policy gradient on retrieved-document tokens.
- Trained on a small mixed-difficulty subset of HotpotQA + 2Wiki (~8.1k samples); generalises to MuSiQue and Bamboogle (OOD) and beats GPT-4o-mini on Bamboogle when augmented with online search.

## Problem

Multi-hop QA agents either rely on prompted scaffolds or on supervised tool-use traces. Both are brittle: prompts do not transfer, and traces are expensive to collect. The paper asks whether outcome-only RL can elicit search behaviour in a base model without any reasoning supervision.

## Method

- **Algorithm**: Reinforce++ with two modifications:
  - **RAG-rollout**: generation pauses at the end-of-query marker; the retriever is called; results are inserted before generation continues. Necessary because the policy and the environment co-produce the trajectory.
  - **Retrieve-mask loss**: tokens that came from the retriever are masked out of the policy gradient so the model is not rewarded or penalised for copying retrieved evidence.
- **Tag schema**: `<begin_of_query> ... <end_of_query>` for queries and `<begin_of_documents> ... <end_of_documents>` for retrieved chunks (different from the `<search>` schema used by Search-R1 and ReSearch). Final answer is unwrapped at the end.
- **Reward**:
  - **Stage 1** (retrieval learning, ~350 samples): retrieval reward 0.5 if the model calls the retriever at least once, else 0; format reward 0.5 for correct tagging, else 0. Capped at 1.0.
  - **Stage 2** (answer accuracy, ~8.1k samples): answer reward = F1 between predicted and gold; format reward is **0 if correct, -2 if incorrect** (asymmetric penalty designed to prevent format collapse late in training).
- **Retrieval setup**: BGE-large-en-v1.5 dense retriever over English Wikipedia from KILT 2019, segmented into 100-word passages with titles (~29M passages). Online evaluation on Bamboogle uses Google Web Search with GPT-4o-mini summaries.
- The paper compares **GRPO vs Reinforce++** in an ablation: GRPO produced longer responses and stronger OOD generalisation, but Reinforce++ won on in-domain. They picked the in-domain winner.

## Training setup

(Pulled from `scripts/qwen_reinforce_plus_train.sh` in the [official repo](https://github.com/RUCAIBox/R1-Searcher); the paper itself does not enumerate compute.)

- **Base models**: `Qwen2.5-7B-Base` and `Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct` (paper §4.1).
- **GPUs**: **8 (type not stated)**. Script sets `CUDA_VISIBLE_DEVICES=0..7`, `--actor_num_gpus_per_node 4`, `--ref_num_gpus_per_node 4`, `--vllm_tensor_parallel_size 2`. The paper does not specify GPU type and the training script does not record it.
- **Distributed config**: ZeRO-2; actor and reference each pinned to 4 GPUs; vLLM TP=2 for rollout. Overlap-comm enabled.
- **Batch sizes**: `train_batch_size=256`, `micro_train_batch_size=1`, `rollout_batch_size=64`, `micro_rollout_batch_size=1`, `n_samples_per_prompt=16` (G=16).
- **Optimisation**: actor LR `2e-6`, critic LR `9e-6`, `init_kl_coef=0.0` (no KL penalty), warmup ratio 0, gradient checkpointing, bf16, FlashAttn, packed samples.
- **Sequence**: `prompt_max_len=1024`, `generate_max_len=29000`.
- **Schedule**: 1 epoch per stage, `num_episodes=10000` cap (effectively dataset-bounded), `save_steps=20`.
- **Wall-clock**: not stated in paper or scripts.
- **Reward server**: separate `reward_server_qwen_zero.py` process serves the F1 + format reward; not co-located with the trainer.

## Data

- **Train (Stage 1)**: 200 medium-difficulty HotpotQA + 150 medium 2Wiki = 350 samples.
- **Train (Stage 2)**: 2,561 medium + 2,000 hard HotpotQA, 1,087 medium + 2,500 hard 2Wiki = 8,148 samples (paper count; some sources also list a small number of "easy" buffer items).
- **Difficulty bucketing**: by initial-policy rollout count to solve. <10 rollouts = easy (excluded), 10-20 = medium, >20 = hard. Hard examples drove most of the gain (3.4 pp CEM ablation effect).
- **Eval**: HotpotQA dev, 2WikiMultiHopQA dev, MuSiQue dev (OOD), Bamboogle (OOD; both offline and Google-Search variants).
- **Metrics**: Cover Exact Match (`ACC_R`) and LLM-as-Judge (`ACC_L`) using GPT-4o-mini. Note: the paper does not headline EM/F1; comparing to ReSearch / Search-R1 numbers requires re-scoring.

## Results

(All numbers are LLM-as-Judge from arXiv 2503.05592v2, Table 3.)

| Benchmark | Qwen2.5-7B-Base | Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct | Best baseline (ReARTeR) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| HotpotQA | 75.0 | 74.6 | ~50.6 |
| 2WikiMultiHopQA | 65.0 | 62.8 | ~53.4 |
| Bamboogle (offline) | 54.4 | 54.4 | ~40 |
| Bamboogle (online, +Google) | "+11.4 pp over Search-o1 32B" | - | - |

Average improvement vs ReARTeR: ~48% on HotpotQA, ~22% on 2Wiki (relative). Beats GPT-4o-mini on Bamboogle online.

## Takeaways for us

- **Two-stage curriculum is the load-bearing trick**, not Reinforce++. Stage 1 solves the cold-start problem we hit in Phase-1 base-model attempts (5/5 of our v1 base runs stayed at 0 tool calls; see [`../report/RESULTS_v1.md`](../report/RESULTS_v1.md), gotcha #2 in [`../../claude/CLAUDE.md`](../../claude/CLAUDE.md)). Worth trying as a **drop-in** before any algorithm change.
- **Asymmetric format penalty** (`+0 / -2`) in Stage 2 is cheap and addresses a failure mode we have not yet faced but might at G=8+. Cheaper than KL.
- **`init_kl_coef = 0.0`** is interesting: paper trains stably without a reference-model anchor. Our [training/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md](../training/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md) currently runs with KL on; an ablation could free reference-model GPU memory.
- **G=16 rollouts** is twice what Search-R1 does and three times ReSearch. On 1× A100 this would multiply rollout cost; not affordable for us at full Search-R1 shape.
- **Compute**: 8 GPUs (type not stated). Our budget is 1 A100. The recipe is not a like-for-like for us; the **shape** transfers (2-stage, asymmetric format reward), the **scale** does not.
- **Tag schema differs** from Search-R1 / ReSearch (`<begin_of_query>` vs `<search>`). Decoupling the format from the policy is something to remember when porting; our [v1 training](../training/CONVERSATION_CONTEXT.md) uses `<tool_call>` already, so we are not bound to either.

This paper does NOT describe the recipe behind our [v0](../report/RESULTS_v0.md) and [v1](../report/RESULTS_v1.md) results. That paper is [ReSearch](2503.19470_research.md). R1-Searcher is the **current focus** for borrowing tricks.

## Limitations

- Authors admit: no scaling beyond 7B; curriculum is "simple data mixing", not pedagogical.
- We add: compute is opaque - GPU type and wall-clock are not stated in paper or scripts. The 8-GPU / G=16 / 29k generate-max combination is not reproducible at our scale even if type is unknown.
- LLM-as-Judge with GPT-4o-mini introduces an evaluation dependency on a closed model. Numbers above are not directly comparable to our EM-only Search-R1 reproduction.
- No reward-design ablation (EM vs CEM vs F1 was tested for **answer reward only**; the format reward shape is not ablated).
- No retrieve-mask ablation; the paper claims it matters but does not measure how much.

## Open questions / followups

- How many steps does Stage 1 actually need to bootstrap tool-use? The script sets `save_steps=20`; an early-stop heuristic on retrieval-rate is implicit. Worth testing on Qwen3.5-2B with our setup; could be the cheapest experiment in the recipe stack.
- Does retrieve-mask matter once the policy has learned not to copy? Cheap to ablate.
- What is the wall-clock per stage on 8×H800? Worth opening an issue on the repo.
- Compare R1-Searcher and ReSearch on a common scoring metric (EM, F1) so we are not comparing LJ to EM.
